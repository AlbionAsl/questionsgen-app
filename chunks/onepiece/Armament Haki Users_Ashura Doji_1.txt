Ashura Doji was a former bandit, a samurai from Wano Country and a retainer of the Kouzuki Family, serving as one of Kouzuki Oden's Nine Red Scabbards. After Oden's death, he returned to being a criminal under the name Shutenmaru, assuming leadership of the Mt. Atama Thieves. He initially severed his ties with the Kouzuki Family in the present but rejoined after witnessing Yasuie's death. Ashura eventually met his demise during the Raid on Onigashima, sacrificing himself to protect his fellow Scabbards from one of Kanjuro's schemes. Ashura Doji, previously a notorious criminal in Kuri, served as a retainer for Kozuki Oden. Following Oden's demise and the loss of his comrades, Ashura became disillusioned and returned to crime, adopting the alias Shutenmaru and leading a band of thieves. Although he initially cut ties with the Kozuki Family, he rejoined after seeing Yasuie's death. Provided by: Fandom Who were the Nine Red Scabbards that Ashura Doji served with? Ashura Doji was a member of the Nine Red Scabbards, a group of samurai serving under Kozuki Oden. The other members included Kin'emon, Denjiro, Kikunojo, Raizo, Inuarashi, Nekomamushi, Kawamatsu, and Izou. Known for their loyalty to Oden, their combined strength and skills enabled them to confront Kaid after two decades of honing their abilities. Provided by: Fandom How did Ashura Doji meet his end during the Raid on Onigashima? During the Onigashima Raid, Ashura Doji, leader of the Mt. Atama Thieves, sacrificed himself to shield the Scabbards from Kanjuro's trap, taking a fatal explosion. Even after discovering their allies absent at Tokage Port, Ashura and the Scabbards remained resolute to continue the raid. Provided by: Fandom What event led Ashura Doji to rejoin the Kouzuki Family? Ashura Doji, once a devoted follower of the Kozuki Family, distanced himself after his allies died attacking Kaidou during Momonosuke's absence. His return to the Kozuki Family's cause, to topple Kaidou, was prompted by Shimotsuki Yasuie's self-sacrifice, which breathed new life into the Kozuki revolution.
